    • Welcome

    Hello everyone! This is an introduction to Topics in Feminism. As described in the course syllabus, created by my professor Dr. Zare. She lists out the course objectives.

    At the end of the semester, students will enhance their understanding of answers to the following questions:


    1. 1. What are the limitations of using “sisterhood is global” as a foundation? How do Western feminists avoid a “save the woman” discourse?

    2. 2. In the realm of religion, the economy, and education, what kinds of ideas about gender appear and how do they change?

    3. 3. How do feminist theory and practice respond to change?

    4. 4. What do effective artistic and musical protests add to the public conversation?

    5. 5. What are dilemmas, possibilities, and strategies of feminism in different geographic areas?

      6. Our course will move through a group of themes, considering events and debates in different countries. There is a special emphasis on India, one of the most internally diverse regions of the world and the second most populous country in the world.


      This is a seminar-style course in which the work centers on readings and in-depth discussion. Time is set aside to help form and maintain a supportive learning community because that is a better environment for students to extend their knowledge and build on a variety of skills.

      Additional Information

      For the past nine years, I have been taking students to South India on a social justice short course. The next opportunity to travel to Hyderabad, India, is in late December 2018. Students in the course have an opportunity to immerse themselves in Indian culture, deepen their knowledge of social justice issues, and learn about creative problem-solving and effective on-the-ground action from social change agents in Hyderabad and Kadapa. Before the trip, students will participate in a service learning project to spread the word about Aarti Home and Voice4Girls, two organizations changing the futures of low-income girls in Andhra Pradesh and Telangana.
